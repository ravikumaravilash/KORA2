Ayurveda

The World's Oldest Living Healthcare System

Ayurveda is more than medicine. It is a complete philosophy of life developed over 5,000 years ago in India, emphasizing harmony between the body, mind, spirit, and environment.

Explore Ayurveda

What is Ayurveda?

The word Ayurveda comes from the Sanskrit words "Ayus" (life) and "Veda" (knowledge), meaning "The Knowledge of Life." Rather than focusing only on curing illness, Ayurveda promotes prevention, balance, healthy living, proper nutrition, exercise, meditation, and natural healing.

5000 Years of History

3000 BCE

Origins of Ayurvedic knowledge through the Vedic civilization.

1500 BCE

Compilation of the Atharva Veda, one of the earliest texts containing medical knowledge.

600 BCE

Charaka and Sushruta developed the foundations of internal medicine and surgery.

Modern Era

Ayurveda is recognised globally and researched alongside modern medicine.

The Three Doshas

Vata

Represents movement, breathing, circulation, and the nervous system.

Pitta

Represents digestion, metabolism, body temperature, and transformation.

Kapha

Represents stability, growth, strength, lubrication, and immunity.

Ayurveda in Modern Healthcare

Today Ayurveda contributes to preventive healthcare, joint rehabilitation, mental wellness, nutrition, pain management, physiotherapy support, and lifestyle medicine. Modern scientific research continues to investigate traditional medicinal plants, herbal compounds, and integrative treatment approaches.

Rapid urbanisation and biodiversity loss threaten medicinal plants and traditional healthcare knowledge. KORA digitises Ayurvedic education, helps preserve cultural heritage, promotes sustainable cultivation, and makes authentic information accessible to future generations.
